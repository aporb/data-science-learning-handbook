# Engagement Targets
## Accounts, Communities, and People to Tag, Follow, and Engage With

**Purpose:** Build the engagement velocity and community network that makes the Federal Data Science Learning Handbook launch succeed. These are not people to cold-pitch — they are people to engage with substantively before, during, and after launch.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS LIST

**Tier 1 (Engage Weekly — Before and During Launch):**
Comment on their posts. Add specific value. Never "great post." Establish yourself in their comment section before tagging them in anything.

**Tier 2 (Engage at Launch — Tag in Announcement or Week 1 Follow-Up):**
Tag only if you have an existing relationship OR if you're tagging them on something genuinely relevant to their work. Cold tagging without context looks like spam.

**Tier 3 (Monitor and Respond):**
Follow these accounts, watch for relevant content, and respond when the handbook is directly relevant to what they're posting about.

## TAGGING PROTOCOL

**In main posts:** Maximum 2-3 tags. Only tag people you have a relationship with or people who contributed to the work.

**In comments:** More flexibility. You can tag people in your own comment thread if their work is directly relevant.

**Never:** Tag people just to get attention. It reads as desperation and can damage the relationship.

**Before any tag:** Ask yourself — "Would this person feel good about being tagged in this?" If uncertain, don't.
