I spent the last year writing the handbook I wish existed when I started doing data science on federal platforms.

It doesn't exist. So I built it.

The Federal Data Science Learning Handbook — 96,000 words, 13 chapters, completely free and open source.

It covers five platforms that federal analysts actually use:

→ Advana (DoD's enterprise data environment)
→ Databricks
→ Qlik
→ Navy Jupiter
→ Palantir AIP / Foundry

This is not a vendor tutorial. This is not a "data science 101" refresher.

This is the stuff that doesn't show up in documentation — the practical, mission-context knowledge that takes junior analysts 18 months of trial and error to figure out on their own.

Here's what frustrated me about the status quo:

We spend billions on federal data platforms. We hire analysts with degrees and certifications. And then we hand them a login and tell them to figure it out.

The average federal data analyst spends their first 6 months learning the platform, not doing the mission work.

That's a capability gap. It's also an absurd waste of money.

This handbook is my attempt to close it — one chapter at a time.

Who is this for:
— Junior analysts onboarding to any of these five platforms
— Team leads building data science practices inside DoD programs
— GovCon firms winning data task orders and needing to stand up teams fast
— Anyone who's ever said "I can't find good training for [federal platform]"

Link in the comments. It's free. Always will be.

If this helps even one team skip the 18-month learning curve, it was worth every hour.
