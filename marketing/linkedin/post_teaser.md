# Pre-Launch Teaser Post
## Build anticipation 7–14 days before announcement

**Format:** Text-only
**Timing:** 10–14 days before main announcement, Tuesday or Wednesday, 8:00–9:00 AM ET
**Target Length:** 900–1,100 characters
**Angle:** Practitioner (personal, grounded, specific — NOT hype)

---

## PRIMARY TEASER POST (Day -14)

---

Something I've been building for a year drops soon.

I'm not going to oversell it. Let me just tell you what it is and who it's for.

It's a handbook. A real one — not a slide deck, not a vendor tutorial, not a "intro to Python" course with a government logo on it.

96,000 words covering data science on five federal platforms that analysts actually work on.

I started writing it because I kept watching smart people join federal data programs and spend their first year just learning the environment. Not doing the work. Learning the environment.

That's not a training problem. That's a documentation gap.

The handbook closes it. Or at least starts to.

More next week.

## CLOSE-IN TEASER (Day -3 — Behind the Origin)

---

This started because I was frustrated.

Three years into working with federal data platforms at IL5, I was still watching analysts struggle with things that should have been documented. Workarounds that had been discovered and rediscovered by dozens of people. Context that got transferred by word of mouth — or not at all when someone rotated out.

The knowledge existed. It just wasn't written down anywhere accessible.

So I started writing it down.

That turned into a chapter. Then another. Then twelve more.

The Federal Data Science Learning Handbook publishes this week.

96,000 words. Five platforms. Free.

If you work in federal data analytics — or you lead a team that does — this is for you.

More details Thursday.

#FederalDataScience #GovCon #OpenSource

## POSTING NOTES FOR ALL TEASERS

- No URLs in any teaser post (no destination to send traffic to yet)
- Resist the urge to over-explain or list features — teasers work through restraint
- The poll post is the most important teaser: it generates engagement velocity and tells you what to lead with on launch day
- If a specific platform dominates the poll, name it in the announcement post's hook
- Keep comments engaged during the teaser week — your responses to comments matter for algorithmic distribution on launch day
