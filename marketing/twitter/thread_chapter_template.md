## TEMPLATE STRUCTURE (6–7 tweets)

---

### Tweet 1 — HOOK

**Formula:** Bold claim + "Here's what I mean:" or numbered list opener

```
[PROVOCATIVE CLAIM ABOUT THE CHAPTER TOPIC].

Most [data scientists / analysts / ML engineers] working in federal environments
get this completely wrong.

Chapter [NUMBER] of the Federal Data Science Learning Handbook fixes that. 🧵

Here's what's inside:
```

**Notes:**
- Lead with the pain point, not the solution
- "Most people get this wrong" outperforms "here's a great resource"
- Keep it under 240 chars

### Tweet 3 — THE FEDERAL REALITY

```
In most federal environments, you're dealing with:

[LIST 3–4 specific, real constraints the reader recognizes immediately]

```

**Example:**
```
In most federal environments, you're dealing with:

- Software whitelisting. Your package manager is not your friend.
- Air-gapped or semi-isolated networks
- Shared computing resources you don't control
- IT approval processes measured in months

Chapter 3 doesn't pretend these don't exist. It's built around them.
```

```
ML in a federal environment doesn't fail at the model.
```
