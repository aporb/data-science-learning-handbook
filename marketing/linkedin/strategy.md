# LinkedIn Launch Strategy: Federal Data Science Learning Handbook
## Author: Amyn Porbanderwala | Director of Innovation | USMC Veteran

---

## Strategic Foundation

### The Asset
The Federal Data Science Learning Handbook is a 96,000-word, 13-chapter open-source resource covering data science on five federal platforms: Advana, Databricks, Qlik, Navy Jupiter, and Palantir AIP/Foundry. This does not exist anywhere else. That's the entire strategy — lead with the gap.

### The Problem It Solves (Anchor This in Every Post)
There is no standard data science curriculum for the federal enterprise. Junior analysts joining programs on Advana or Palantir Foundry learn by tribal knowledge, vendor documentation written for commercial contexts, or not at all. This handbook changes that.

### Brand Angles (Rotate Across Posts — Never Combine All Three in One)
1. **Practitioner** — "Here's what I built and how it works" (specific, tool-level)
2. **Thought Leader** — "Here's what this means for the federal enterprise" (systems-level)
3. **Industry Challenger** — "This gap should embarrass all of us" (provocative, data-backed)

- Post 2x per week minimum
- Rotate through 13 chapter spotlights (one per post cycle)
- Intersperse with platform spotlights (5 platforms = 5 posts over 5 weeks)
- Every 3rd post: industry challenge or data-backed provocation

**Week 2-3:** Chapters 1-4 + Advana platform spotlight
**Week 3-4:** Chapters 5-7 + Databricks platform spotlight
**Week 4-5:** Chapters 8-10 + Qlik platform spotlight
**Week 5-6:** Chapters 11-12 + Navy Jupiter spotlight

**Week 7-8:** Retrospective + "What I'd add to Volume 2" post

### Phase 3: Sustained Authority (Month 2+)
**Goal:** Become the known voice on federal data science training and GovCon productization.

- 1-2 posts per week maintaining chapter/platform content

## Hashtag Strategy

LinkedIn's 360Brew AI engine rewards topic authority over hashtag volume. Use 3-5 targeted hashtags max. Never more.

### Primary Hashtags (Use on every handbook-related post)
- #FederalDataScience
- #GovCon
- #DataScience

### Rotation Hashtags (Mix in based on post topic)
- #DoD (DoD-specific posts)
- #Palantir (platform posts)
- #Databricks (platform posts)
- #FederalTech
- #DigitalTransformation
- #AI (use sparingly — high competition, low specificity)
- #USMC or #VeteranInTech (personal story posts)
- #OpenSource (for the free/open nature of the handbook)
- #DataEngineering
- #GovConIT

### Hashtags to Avoid
- #Innovation (too generic, too saturated)
- #Leadership (algorithmic noise)
- #Success (zero specificity)
- Any hashtag with under 5,000 followers unless extremely niche and intentional

## Posting Cadence and Timing

### Frequency
- Pre-launch: 1 post every 2-3 days
- Launch week: Daily
- Post-launch: 2-3x per week for 8 weeks, then 1-2x per week

### Best Times to Post (Eastern Time, B2G Audience)
- Tuesday–Thursday: 8:00–9:30 AM ET (federal workers at desk, pre-meetings)
- Tuesday–Thursday: 12:00–1:00 PM ET (lunch scroll)
- Avoid Friday afternoon and Monday morning
- Never post on federal holidays

### The Golden Hour Rule
Within the first 60 minutes of posting:
1. Reply to every comment with a substantive response (10+ words minimum)
2. Add a follow-up comment on your own post with the direct link and one additional insight
3. Do not edit the post after publishing (it resets algorithmic distribution)

- Reply to all comments on handbook posts within 24 hours for 60 days

- If media or influencers engage, send a connection request immediately with a personalized note

### Community Building
- Create a poll in week 2 asking which chapter people want covered first in a follow-up video
- Ask for testimonials from anyone who finds it useful — post those as separate updates
- Offer to be a guest on GovCon and federal tech podcasts (reference handbook as the credibility anchor)

## Content Principles

### Voice and Tone (Non-Negotiable)
- Write like a senior practitioner explaining something to a peer — not down, not up
- Use specific numbers, platform names, and real friction points
- Short paragraphs. One sentence = one idea.
- Never use "I'm excited to announce" — it signals marketing copy, kills authenticity
- Lead with the tension or the problem. The handbook is the resolution.
- Opinionated but not reckless. Back every bold claim with evidence or experience.

### What Not to Do
- Do not beg for likes or shares
- Do not use generic stock photo language ("in today's digital landscape...")
- Do not pitch the book "Shrink-Wrap It" — let the bio do that work
- Do not over-tag people who haven't agreed to be mentioned
- Do not post and disappear — engagement velocity in hour one determines reach
